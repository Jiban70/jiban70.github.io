Understanding Your Loan Amortization

Choosing a loan in 2026 requires more than just looking at the interest rate. Our tool uses the Standard Reducing Balance Method to show exactly how your payments are split between principal repayment and interest costs over time.

The EMI Mathematical Formula

To calculate Equated Monthly Installments, the following actuarial formula is applied:

$$EMI = \frac{P \cdot r \cdot (1 + r)^n}{(1 + r)^n - 1}$$

Where $P$ is the principal amount, $r$ is the monthly interest rate (annual rate/12), and $n$ is the total number of months.

Key Terms to Know

  • Principal: The actual amount you borrowed from the bank.
  • Interest: The cost of borrowing, which is highest in the first year of the loan.
  • Amortization: The process of spreading out a loan into a series of fixed payments over time.

Why Interest is Front-Loaded

In the early stages of a loan, your monthly payments are mostly interest. This is because interest is calculated based on your remaining balance. As you pay off more principal, the interest portion shrinks, and more of your money goes toward owning the asset. This is clearly visible in our Amortization Schedule above.
