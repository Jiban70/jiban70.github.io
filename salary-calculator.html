Understanding Your 2026 Paycheck

Whether you are starting a new job or negotiating a raise, knowing your Net Income is more important than your Gross Salary. The Gross amount is what is written in your contract, but the Net amount is what actually lands in your bank account after federal taxes, social security, and health insurance premiums are deducted.

Gross vs. Net Income: What's the Difference?

The "Tax & Deductions" field in our calculator allows you to input your specific local withholding rate. In 2026, most professional employees see a total deduction range of 18% to 35% depending on their tax bracket and benefits like 401k or pension contributions.

How to Convert Hourly to Annual Salary

To calculate your annual salary from an hourly rate, the standard formula is:
Hourly Rate × Hours per Week × 52 Weeks = Annual Gross Salary.
Our tool automates this process, allowing you to switch between periods instantly to see how your pay scales across days, weeks, and months.
