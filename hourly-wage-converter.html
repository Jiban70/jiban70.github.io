How to Calculate Your Annual Salary

Converting your hourly wage to a yearly salary involves more than just multiplying by 2,000. To get a precise figure, you must account for the actual number of workdays in the year, potential overtime, and any unpaid time off.

The Precise Formula

Our calculator uses the standard 52-week fiscal model while allowing for negative adjustments for unpaid leave. The formula applied is:

Income = (Base × Hours) + (OT_Hours × Base × Multiplier) × 52 - (Unpaid_Days × 8 × Base)

Understanding Overtime Impact

In many regions, hours worked over 40 in a single week are subject to a higher pay rate—typically 1.5 times the base rate. Even a small amount of consistent overtime can significantly boost your annual gross income. For example, working just 5 hours of overtime a week at a $30/hr base rate adds over $11,000 to your yearly total.

Why Daily Rate Matters

The daily rate is calculated based on 261 working days (the average number of weekdays in a year). Knowing your daily worth helps when evaluating whether to take a day of unpaid leave or when negotiating project-based consulting rates.

Budgeting Tips for Hourly Workers

  • The 52-Week Rule: Always base your annual budget on 52 weeks, but keep a "rainy day" fund for weeks where hours might be cut.
  • Tax Withholding: Remember that these figures are Gross Income (before taxes). Your actual take-home pay will be lower depending on your tax bracket.
  • Paid Time Off (PTO): If your job offers PTO, you don't need to enter "Unpaid Days" for your vacation time, as you are still being paid for those hours.
