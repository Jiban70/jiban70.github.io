Professional Pricing Strategies

In competitive retail, understanding the math behind your price tag is vital for profitability. Our Sale Price Calculator provides two essential modes for business owners and savvy shoppers.

1. Markup Mode (For Business Owners)

Markup is the amount added to the cost price of goods to cover overheads and profit. Our engine calculates the final selling price based on your cost and desired markup percentage. It also provides your Profit Margin—a key metric often confused with markup. Margin represents profit as a percentage of the selling price, whereas Markup is a percentage of the cost.

[Image: Illustration comparing Markup vs Margin]

2. Reverse Mode (For Savvy Shoppers & Liquidation)

Have you ever seen a sale for 25% off but wanted to know what the price was originally? Reverse calculation is perfect for verifying discounts or determining the original MSRP. Our formula ensures that if an item is $75 after a 25% discount, it correctly identifies the original $100 price point.

Pricing Formulas Used:

  • Sale Price: Cost + (Cost × Markup %)
  • Profit Margin: ((Sale Price - Cost) / Sale Price) × 100
  • Original Price (Reverse): Sale Price / (1 - Discount %)

Business Tip: To maintain a 20% profit margin, you actually need to apply a 25% markup to your cost. Always check your margin after setting markups to ensure your business remains sustainable.
